Sleep vs. performance

Why this matters

Sleep is one of the biggest — and most controllable — drivers of athletic performance: reaction time, decision-making, recovery and mood all move with it. This chart plots each day's sleep against how you performed, so you can see whether more sleep actually tracks with better days for you, not just in general. The correlation r in the headline sums up how tightly the two move together (−1 to +1).

How performance is scored

On a match day, we use your own self-rated performance (1–10).

On every other day there's no single rating, so we blend that day's logged metrics into one 0–10 score. Each metric is converted so higher always means better, then combined as a weighted average:

  • Mood after — 20%
  • Confidence — 15%
  • Focus — 15%
  • Sleep quality — 12%
  • Stress (lower is better) — 12%
  • Sleep hours (best around 8h) — 10%
  • Body soreness (lower is better) — 8%
  • Screen time (lower is better) — 8%

Metrics you didn't log that day are skipped and the rest reweighted, so a lighter log still scores fairly. Training load (intensity and duration) is deliberately left out — a hard, long session shouldn't count as a worse day.
